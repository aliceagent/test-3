# Torah Light (Torah之光) — Content Creation Plan

## Overview

This plan outlines how to create comprehensive text content for every section of the Torah Light website, sourcing primarily from [Chabad.org](https://www.chabad.org), [Aish.com](https://aish.com), and [Sefaria.org](https://www.sefaria.org/texts). Content must be written in all three languages (English, Chinese Mandarin, Hebrew) and follow Orthodox Jewish perspectives.

**Important**: All content should be original text *inspired by* and *referencing* these sources — not copied verbatim. Each page should link back to the source sites for deeper study.

---

## Part 1: New Categories to Add

The following sections are **missing from the current site** and should be added:

### 1. Weekly Parsha (פרשת השבוע) — `weekly-parsha`
**Category**: Foundations
**Why**: This is the heartbeat of Jewish learning. Every week, Jews worldwide study the same Torah portion. A weekly dvar Torah section for the Shabbat table is essential — especially for Chinese families new to Judaism who want a structured way to bring Torah into their home.
**Content sources**:
- [Chabad.org Parshah](https://www.chabad.org/parshah/default_cdo/jewish/Torah-Portion.htm) — summaries, commentaries, stories
- [Aish.com Weekly Torah Portion](https://aish.com/weekly-torah-portion/) — beginner, advanced, and family levels
- [Torah.org Parsha Halacha for the Shabbos Table](https://torah.org/series/parsha-halacha/) — family discussion format
- [Aleph Beta](https://www.alephbeta.org/weekly-torah-portion) — clear, evidence-based approach

**Content structure per parsha**:
- Parsha name (Hebrew, English, Chinese)
- Summary of the portion (3-5 paragraphs)
- Key themes and lessons
- "At the Shabbat Table" — 3 discussion questions for families
- A short dvar Torah (suitable for sharing at meals)
- Connection to daily life

### 2. Brachot & Daily Blessings (ברכות) — `blessings`
**Category**: Daily Living
**Why**: Blessings are among the first practices a new observant Jew learns. Knowing what bracha to say on which food, when waking up, before and after meals, etc., is fundamental daily practice.
**Content sources**:
- [Chabad.org Blessings](https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/926424/jewish/Blessings.htm)
- [Aish.com Prayer & Blessings](https://aish.com/atr/prayer-blessings/)
- [Sefaria — Siddur](https://www.sefaria.org/texts/Liturgy)

### 3. Mezuzah, Tefillin & Tzitzit (מזוזה, תפילין, ציצית) — `mitzvah-objects`
**Category**: Daily Living
**Why**: These three mitzvot are highly visible markers of Jewish life and often the first ritual objects a new Jew acquires. The current site has no dedicated coverage.
**Content sources**:
- [Chabad.org Tefillin](https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/1918252/jewish/Tefillin.htm)
- [Chabad.org Mezuzah](https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/256915/jewish/Mezuzah.htm)
- [Chabad.org Tzitzit](https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/530126/jewish/Tzitzit.htm)

### 4. Conversion to Judaism (גיור) — `conversion`
**Category**: Community & Life
**Why**: Extremely relevant for the target audience. Many Chinese learners are exploring Judaism with the possibility of conversion. This is one of the most important missing sections.
**Content sources**:
- [Aish.com Conversion](https://aish.com/atr/conversion/)
- [Chabad.org Conversion](https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/3002/jewish/Conversion-to-Judaism.htm)

**Content structure**:
- What is conversion (giyur)?
- The process: study, beit din, mikvah, (brit milah for men)
- How long does it take?
- Finding a sponsoring rabbi
- Orthodox vs. other conversions
- Living as a ger/giyoret (convert)
- Famous converts in Jewish history (Ruth, Onkelos, etc.)
- Resources for converts in Asia

### 5. Pirkei Avot — Ethics of the Fathers (פרקי אבות) — `pirkei-avot`
**Category**: Texts & Philosophy
**Why**: Universally studied, especially between Pesach and Rosh Hashana. Accessible, practical wisdom perfect for newcomers.
**Content sources**:
- [Sefaria — Pirkei Avot](https://www.sefaria.org/Pirkei_Avot)
- [Chabad.org — Ethics of the Fathers](https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/2165/jewish/Ethics-of-the-Fathers.htm)

**Content structure**:
- All 6 chapters with translation (English, Chinese, Hebrew)
- Commentary on each mishnah
- Practical applications

### 6. Israel & the Holy Land (ארץ ישראל) — `israel`
**Category**: History & Identity
**Why**: Central to Jewish identity, prayer, and eschatology. Important for Chinese Jews who may never have visited.
**Content sources**:
- [Chabad.org — Israel](https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/3610/jewish/Israel.htm)
- [Aish.com — Israel](https://aish.com/israel/)

### 7. Mussar — Character Development (מוסר) — `mussar`
**Category**: Texts & Philosophy
**Why**: Practical ethical self-improvement. Resonates deeply with Chinese culture's emphasis on self-cultivation (修身).
**Content sources**:
- [Sefaria — Mussar texts](https://www.sefaria.org/texts)
- Mesillat Yesharim, Orchot Tzaddikim, Chovot HaLevavot

### 8. The Jewish Calendar (לוח השנה) — `jewish-calendar`
**Category**: Daily Living
**Why**: Understanding the Hebrew months, how dates work, when holidays fall, and the rhythm of the Jewish year is foundational.
**Content sources**:
- [Chabad.org — Jewish Calendar](https://www.chabad.org/calendar/view/month.htm)
- [Aish.com — Calendar](https://aish.com/atr/jewish-calendar/)

## Part 3: Content Creation Process

### Step 1: Research & Outline (per page)
1. Read 3-5 articles on the topic from Chabad.org, Aish.com, and Sefaria
2. Create an outline with all sections and key points
3. Identify Hebrew terms that need transliteration + Chinese translation
4. Note relevant halachic sources

### Step 2: Write English Version
1. Write clear, accessible English text (target: 8th grade reading level)
2. Include Hebrew terms with transliteration on first use
3. Add "Did You Know?" boxes with interesting facts
4. Add "Practical Steps" sections for actionable content
5. Include source references (linking to Sefaria, Chabad.org, Aish.com)

### Step 3: Chinese (Mandarin) Translation
1. Translate from English, adapting cultural references for Chinese readers
2. Include pinyin for specialized Jewish terms on first use
3. Add cultural bridges (e.g., comparing Shabbat rest to Chinese concepts of balance)
4. Ensure Hebrew terms have consistent Chinese transliterations throughout the site

### Step 4: Hebrew Version
1. Write/translate Hebrew content
2. Ensure RTL formatting works correctly
3. Include nikud (vowel marks) for difficult words

### Step 5: Review
1. Halachic review by a qualified rabbi
2. Chinese language review by native Mandarin speaker
3. Technical review for correct rendering in all three languages

## Part 5: Content Specifications

### Per-Page Target
- **Word count**: 2,000–5,000 words per page (English)
- **Sections**: 5–12 sections per page
- **Hebrew text**: Include original Hebrew for all prayers, blessings, and key verses
- **Transliteration**: Ashkenazi pronunciation (matching Chabad convention)
- **Links**: 3–5 external links to source sites per page
- **Cross-links**: 2–4 links to other Torah Light pages

### Consistent Elements Across All Pages
1. **Page title** (Hebrew name + English + Chinese)
2. **Introduction paragraph** (what this topic is and why it matters)
3. **Table of Contents** (auto-generated from sections)
4. **Main content sections** with clear headings
5. **"For the Shabbat Table"** callout — a discussion question or short thought related to the topic (where applicable)
6. **"First Steps"** — practical beginner action items
7. **"Learn More"** — links to Chabad.org, Aish.com, Sefaria
8. **Related Topics** — links to other Torah Light pages

### Style Guide
- Warm, welcoming tone (not academic)
- Assume the reader knows nothing — explain every term
- Use "we" language to include the reader in the Jewish community
- Avoid denominational politics — present Orthodox perspective clearly but respectfully
- Chinese translations should feel natural, not like translated English
- Include romanized Hebrew (transliteration) for all Hebrew terms
