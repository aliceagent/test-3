# Bibliography and Sources

## Primary Sources

1. **Hebrew Bible**
   - Exodus 20:2-17 (עשרת הדברות - First Version)
   - Deuteronomy 5:6-21 (עשרת הדברות - Second Version)
   - Deuteronomy 4:13
   - Genesis 2:2-3

## Classical Jewish Commentaries

2. **Rashi (1040-1105)**
   - Commentary on Exodus 20
   - Commentary on Deuteronomy 5

3. **Ramban/Nachmanides (1194-1270)**
   - Commentary on the Torah, Exodus 20
   - Commentary on Deuteronomy 5

4. **Ibn Ezra (1089-1167)**
   - Commentary on Exodus 20:1
   - Long commentary on the Decalogue differences

5. **Sa'adia Gaon/Rasag (882-942)**
   - Commentary on differences between tablets (cited in Ibn Ezra)

6. **Talmud Bavli**
   - Tractate Berakhot 20a (זכור ושמור - Remember and Observe)
   - Tractate Tamid 5:1 (Temple recitation of Decalogue)

## Archaeological Sources

11. **Dead Sea Scrolls**
    - 4QDeutn (Cave 4, Qumran) - Deuteronomy manuscript with Decalogue
    - Qumran Tefillin containing mixed Decalogue texts

12. **Nash Papyrus**
    - 2nd century BCE Hebrew manuscript (Egypt) 
    - Contains mixed version of Decalogue

## Historical Context

13. **Mishnah**
    - Tractate Tamid 5.1 (Daily Temple recitation)
    - References to עֲשֶׂרֶת הַדִּבְּרוֹת (Aseret ha-Dibrot)

14. **Septuagint (LXX)**
    - Greek translation variations in Exodus and Deuteronomy
    - Textual differences from Masoretic Text

15. **Samaritan Pentateuch**
    - Alternative textual tradition
    - Variations in Decalogue ordering

## Modern Scholarship

16. **Wikipedia Entry**
    - "Ten Commandments" 
      URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ten_Commandments
    - Contains comprehensive overview of textual differences

17. **Philo of Alexandria (20 BCE - 50 CE)**
    - "On the Decalogue" (De Decalogo)
    - Early Jewish-Hellenistic interpretation

## Chinese Philosophical Parallels

18. **Wang Yangming (1472-1529)**
    - Theory of 知行合一 (Unity of Knowledge and Action)
    - Ming Dynasty Neo-Confucian philosophy

19. **Song Dynasty Neo-Confucianism**
    - 理气关系 (Relationship between Li and Qi)
    - Principle and material manifestation concepts
