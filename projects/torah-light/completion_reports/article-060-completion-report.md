## Content Summary

This article provides comprehensive guidance for Jews or prospective converts living in Asia on how to find rabbis, connect with Jewish communities, and build meaningful relationships even in remote locations.

### Key Topics Covered

1. **The Importance of Having a Rabbi**
   - Definition of a rabbi in Judaism (teacher, mentor, guide)
   - The Talmudic teaching "Make for yourself a rabbi" (Pirkei Avot 1:6)
   - What to look for in choosing a personal rabbi

2. **Chabad Houses Across Asia**
   - Detailed directory of Chabad centers in China (Beijing, Shanghai, Hong Kong, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Chengdu, etc.)
   - Chabad presence in other Asian countries (Thailand, Singapore, Japan, Korea, Taiwan, India, Vietnam, Philippines)
   - How to connect with Chabad houses

3. **Virtual Learning & Online Rabbis**
   - Aish Academy interactive courses
   - ChabadU video classes
   - Partners in Torah one-on-one matching
   - Online "Ask the Rabbi" services

4. **The Example of Ruth**
   - Ruth's declaration (Ruth 1:16-17) as the model for joining the Jewish people
   - What Ruth had: a teacher (Naomi), commitment, and community
   - Ruth as great-grandmother of King David

5. **Practical Step-by-Step Roadmap**
   - Week 1: Research options
   - Weeks 2-3: Make initial contact
   - Weeks 4-8: Attend services
   - Months 2-6: Build relationship with rabbi
   - Ongoing: Commit to regular learning
   - If applicable: Formal conversion process

6. **Cultural Bridge: Chinese Teacher-Student Tradition**
   - Chinese 师徒关系 (teacher-student bond) parallels Jewish rav-talmid relationship
   - Quote: "一日为师，终身为父" (One day as teacher, lifetime as father)
   - Confucian teaching: "三人行，必有我师焉" (Among three people, there is my teacher)
