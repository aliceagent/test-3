## Content Summary

This comprehensive trilingual article explores the Western Wall (Kotel), the holiest accessible site in Judaism and the last remnant of the Second Temple complex destroyed in 70 CE.

### Major Topics Covered

1. **Temple History**
   - First Temple built by King Solomon (~1000 BCE / Western Zhou Dynasty)
   - Babylonian destruction (586 BCE / Spring & Autumn period)
   - Second Temple and Herodian expansion
   - Roman destruction (70 CE / Eastern Han Dynasty)
   - Massive retaining walls that survived

2. **Theological Significance**
   - Closest accessible point to the Holy of Holies
   - Divine Presence (Shekhinah) never departed
   - The "Wailing Wall" - tears and prayers across generations
   - "Gate of Heaven" where prayers ascend directly

3. **Historical Journey Through the Ages**
   - Byzantine and early Islamic periods (637 CE / Tang Dynasty)
   - Crusader restrictions
   - Mamluk and Ottoman rule
   - British Mandate and Jordanian control (1948-1967)
   - Six-Day War liberation (1967) - "Temple Mount is in our hands!"
   - Creation of the modern plaza

4. **Visiting Customs and Practices**
   - Plaza divided into men's and women's sections
   - Walking toward the Wall facing forward, backward when leaving
   - Touching, kissing, or pressing foreheads against ancient stones
   - Placing written prayers (kvitlach) in the cracks
   - Bar mitzvahs and prayer services
   - Modest dress requirements

5. **Western Wall Tunnels**
   - Underground archaeological discoveries
   - Full height and extent of Herodian construction
   - Closest point to where Holy of Holies stood
   - Some of the largest building blocks in history

6. **Theological Meaning**
   - Dual symbolism: memory of destruction + hope for redemption
   - The Wall as "gate of heaven"
   - Place of miracles and answered prayers

7. **Chinese Cultural Parallels**
   - **Sacred mountains:** Mount Tai (泰山) parallel to Temple Mount as heaven-earth meeting point
   - **Scholar's rocks (供石):** Stones holding memory and spiritual energy
   - **Great Wall continuity:** Symbol of unbroken heritage across dynasties
   - **Pilgrimage traditions:** Journey itself as spiritual practice

8. **Modern Role**
   - National symbol of Jewish people
   - IDF swearing-in ceremonies
   - 24-hour live stream worldwide
   - Place of unity across all Jewish denominations

9. **Practical Visitor Information**
   - Location in Old City / Jewish Quarter
   - Open 24/7 with security
   - What to bring/not bring
   - Western Wall Tunnels tours
