# Chabad.org: How to Convert to Judaism

**URL**: https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/2972927/jewish/How-to-Convert-to-Judaism.htm

## Key Points

### What Conversion Means
- **Permanent**: Conversion is a one-way street. Once you convert, you're Jewish forever
- **Life commitment**: Not something to take lightly
- **Complete identity change**: The Talmud compares a convert to a newborn child

### The Three Components of Conversion

1. **Accepting the yoke of commandments** (kabbalat mitzvot)
   - Verbal commitment to live according to all Torah commandments
   - Must be before a beit din (rabbinical court of three learned, observant rabbis)

2. **Immersion in the mikvah** (ritual bath)
   - For both men and women
   - Symbolic spiritual rebirth

3. **Circumcision** (for males)
   - If already circumcised, symbolic drawing of blood (hatafat dam brit)
   - Historical precedent from Sinai (Exodus 12:48)

### Why Does It Take So Long?

- Conversion is a **lifetime commitment** requiring deep knowledge and practice
- Candidates must demonstrate:
  - Understanding of Jewish law
  - Commitment to observance
  - Integration into Jewish community life
- Timeline varies by beit din but typically 1+ years
- Process overseen by sponsoring rabbi who vouches for sincerity

### Biblical Basis for Conversion Process

The conversion requirements mirror the Jewish people's "conversion" at Mount Sinai:

1. **Circumcision**: Exodus 12:48 - required before the Exodus
2. **Immersion**: Exodus 19:10 - Moses had the people immerse before receiving Torah
3. **Accepting commandments**: Exodus 24:7 - "We will do and we will hear"
4. **Sacrifice**: Exodus 24:5 - brought offerings (no longer done since Temple destruction)
5. **Beit Din**: Deuteronomy 1:16 - presence of witnesses/judges

### Spiritual Perspective

- Jewish mysticism teaches converts have always had a Jewish soul
- The soul "remembers" its origin and is drawn back
- Like childbirth: painful, long process but worth the result

### Requirements During Study Period

- Must live within walking distance of Orthodox synagogue (Shabbat requirement)
- Access to kosher food
- Learning Hebrew (helpful but not required before starting)
- Integration into community
- Regular study with rabbi/classes

### Finding a Beit Din

- Must be Orthodox and recognized
- When looking, verify:
  - Rabbis are Torah-observant
  - Recognized by established Jewish communities
  - Not self-appointed or fringe groups
- Major conversion courts: Manhattan Beit Din for Conversions, London Beit Din, etc.

### Common Challenges Mentioned in Comments

1. **Geographic isolation**: Many prospective converts live far from Orthodox communities
2. **Expense**: Living in Orthodox neighborhoods (walking distance to shul) is often expensive
3. **Finding sponsoring rabbi**: Can be intimidating to approach
4. **Family relationships**: Navigating non-Jewish family dynamics
5. **Loneliness**: Entering Jewish life without Jewish family
6. **Partner concerns**: Finding spouse in small communities

### What Conversion Is NOT

- Not just intellectual agreement with Judaism
- Not a "club" you can join casually
- Not reversible
- Not about ethnicity (you become fully Jewish regardless of origin)
