# Web Research - Rosh Hashana Customs and Traditions

## Key Customs Found:

### 1. Shofar Blowing
- Blown each day during month of Elul in preparation
- Central symbol of the holiday
- Spiritual "wake-up call"
- Referenced in Rosh Hashana Kiddush
- Ram's horn most commonly used

### 2. Tashlich Ceremony  
- Performed on first afternoon of Rosh Hashana (if not Shabbat)
- Go to body of water (ocean, river, pond)
- Ceremonially cast sins into water
- Some throw bread or pebbles to symbolize "casting off" sins
- Traditional prayer: "Who is like unto you, O God..."
- If first day is Shabbat, postponed to second day (Ashkenazi custom)

### 3. Symbolic Foods
- **Apples and honey**: symbolize desire for sweet new year
- **Head of fish or lamb**: hope to be at "head" not "tail" in new year
- Various simanim (symbolic foods) eaten

### 4. Special Prayers
- **Unetaneh Tokef**: describes day of judgment
- **Avinu Malkeinu**: asks for compassion, forgiveness, blessings
- **Kiddush**: includes reference to shofar

### 5. Timing and Calendar
- Falls on 1st day of Hebrew month Tishrei
- Corresponds to September or October
- Two-day holiday
- Example dates: September 12-13, 2026

### 6. Spiritual Themes
- Day of judgment  
- God reviews behavior and decrees fate for coming year
- Preparation and evaluation
- Great love God has for His people
- Time for repentance and spiritual renewal
