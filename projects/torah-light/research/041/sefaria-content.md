# Sefaria Research - Rosh Hashana Torah Sources

## Key Biblical Sources

### Leviticus 23:24-25 - The Torah's Command for Rosh Hashana
> "Speak to the Israelite people thus: In the seventh month, on the first day of the month, you shall observe complete rest, a sacred occasion commemorated with loud blasts. You shall not work at your occupations; and you shall bring an offering by fire to God."

**Hebrew:** בַּחֹ֨דֶשׁ הַשְּׁבִיעִ֜י בְּאֶחָ֣ד לַחֹ֗דֶשׁ יִהְיֶ֤ה לָכֶם֙ שַׁבָּת֔וֹן זִכְר֥וֹן תְּרוּעָ֖ה מִקְרָא־קֹֽדֶשׁ

**Key Points:**
- Called "זכרון תרועה" (Zichron Teruah) - "remembrance of blowing/shouting"
- Seventh month = Tishrei
- Complete rest like Shabbat
- Sacred occasion with special offerings

### Numbers 29:1 - "Yom Teruah" 
> "In the seventh month, on the first day of the month, you shall observe a sacred occasion: you shall not work at your occupations. You shall observe it as a day when the horn is sounded."

**Hebrew:** י֥וֹם תְּרוּעָ֖ה יִהְיֶ֥ה לָכֶֽם

**Key Points:**
- Explicitly called "Yom Teruah" - "Day of Blowing/Sounding"
- Connected to horn (shofar) blowing
- Sacred day with work prohibition

### Psalms 81:4 - Connection to Shofar
> "Blow the horn on the new moon, on the full moon for our feast day."

**Hebrew:** תִּקְע֣וּ בַחֹ֣דֶשׁ שׁוֹפָ֑ר בַּ֝כֵּ֗סֶה לְי֣וֹם חַגֵּֽנוּ

**Key Points:**
- Direct command to blow shofar
- Connected to new moon (Rosh Hashana)
- Links to "our feast day"
