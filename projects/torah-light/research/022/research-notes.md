# Article #22 Research Notes
## "What Can and Can't I Do on Shabbat? Common Questions Answered"

### Key Topics to Cover (Common Questions):

#### 1. ELECTRICITY
- **Can I turn lights on/off?** NO - violates the prohibition against completing a circuit
- **Can I use a timer?** YES - if set before Shabbat
- **Can I use appliances that were left on?** Sometimes - depends on the appliance
- **Can I open a refrigerator?** YES - but light must be disabled or covered
- **Can I use an elevator?** Special Shabbat elevators only (stops at every floor automatically)

#### 2. PHONES & ELECTRONICS
- **Can I use my phone?** NO - multiple prohibitions (electricity, writing, creating)
- **Can I answer the doorbell?** NO
- **Can I use a computer?** NO
- **Can I listen to music?** NO - if it requires turning on electronics
- **What about a hearing aid?** Permitted if turned on before Shabbat (medical necessity)

#### 3. COOKING & FOOD PREPARATION
- **Can I cook on Shabbat?** NO - one of the 39 melachot
- **Can I reheat food?** YES - if already cooked, on a blech (hotplate) or in a warming oven
- **Can I make tea/coffee?** PARTIALLY - instant only, with water from urn heated before Shabbat
- **Can I use a slow cooker?** YES - if started before Shabbat
- **Can I open a can?** NO - creating an opening
- **Can I cut vegetables?** YES - shortly before eating (not for later)

#### 4. WRITING & CREATING
- **Can I write?** NO - including typing, texting, drawing
- **Can I tear toilet paper?** NO - prepare before Shabbat or use pre-cut
- **Can I open mail?** NO - tearing is prohibited
- **Can I color/draw?** NO

#### 5. CARRYING & MUKTZEH
- **Can I carry items outside?** Only within an eruv
- **What is muktzeh?** Items designated for prohibited uses (money, pens, phones, etc.)
- **Can I move a chair?** YES - unless it's clearly muktzeh
- **Can I carry a tissue?** YES - but only within an eruv or attached to clothing

#### 6. TRANSPORTATION
- **Can I drive?** NO - violates multiple prohibitions
- **Can I ride a bike?** NO
- **Can I ride in a car driven by non-Jew?** Generally NO (except life-threatening emergency)

#### 7. PERSONAL CARE
- **Can I shower?** Complicated - cold water yes, hot water prepared before Shabbat possibly
- **Can I brush my teeth?** Disputed - many are lenient with water only
- **Can I use soap?** Disputed - liquid soap more lenient than bar soap
- **Can I comb my hair?** YES - but gently to avoid pulling out hair
- **Can I cut nails?** NO

#### 8. EMERGENCY SITUATIONS
- **What about medical emergencies?** Pikuach nefesh (saving a life) overrides ALL Shabbat laws
- **Can I call an ambulance?** YES - for life-threatening situations
- **What about non-emergency medical issues?** Ask a rabbi - grey areas exist

### Chinese Cultural Parallels:
- **Rest as sacred obligation** - similar to Confucian emphasis on ritual propriety and proper timing (禮 li)
- **Historical context**: During China's Zhou Dynasty (周朝, ~1046-256 BCE), when the detailed laws of Shabbat were being codified in the Oral Torah, Chinese philosophers were also developing elaborate codes of proper conduct
- **Restraint as spiritual practice** - Taoist concept of wu wei (無為, "non-action") parallels Shabbat's cessation from creative work
- **Family unity through shared practice** - Confucian emphasis on family harmony mirrors Shabbat's role in bringing families together
