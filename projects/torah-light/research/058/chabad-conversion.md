I’m planning to convert to Judaism. Conversion to Judaism has been something I’ve been considering and mulling over for years, and I’ve made my decision. What should I expect? What will the process look like?
Answer:
The most important thing is that when you convert you will be Jewish. This is something that you will carry with you for the rest of your life. It is important to note that conversion to Judaism is a one-way street. Once you take the plunge (literally and figuratively), you’re a full-fledged member of the tribe forever.

The Torah is our foundation, so that would be your first step. Buy a Jewish Hebrew-English copy of the Tanakh (Torah, plus Prophets and Writings) and immerse yourself in it. "Ashkenazi" just means that a Jew's family lived in Ashkenaz, i.e. Germany. It came to refer to Jews who had settled in Eastern and Central Europe and who developed their own culture that differs from that of Jews who settled elsewhere. There's no such thing as "the Ashkenazi faith"; maybe you want to do some more research on what Judaism actually is before making plans to convert to it? And don't forget that you can always become a Noahide--not necessary to do any type of formal conversion.
