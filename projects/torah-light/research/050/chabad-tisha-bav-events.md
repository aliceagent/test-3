# Chabad.org - What Happened on Tisha B'Av

**Source:** https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/946703/jewish/What-Is-Tisha-BAv-Ninth-of-Av.htm

## Seven Major Tragedies on the 9th of Av:

### 1. The Spies Returned With a Bad Report (1313 BCE)
- Israelites in wilderness after Exodus from Egypt
- 12 spies sent to scout Promised Land
- Returned on 9th of Av with fearful report
- People wept that night and refused to enter the land
- God decreed that generation would wander 40 years and die in desert
- Talmud: God said "You wept needlessly that night, I will establish for you a true tragedy over which there will be weeping in future generations" (Taanit 29a)

### 2. First Temple Destroyed (423 BCE)
- Built by King Solomon, stood for 410 years
- Destroyed by Nebuchadnezzar and Babylonians
- Date: 9th of Av
- Jews exiled to Babylon

### 3. Second Temple Destroyed (70 CE)
- Rebuilt 350 years after first destruction
- Stood for 420 years
- Destroyed by Romans in 70 CE
- Date: 9th of Av
- Beginning of long exile (galut)

### 4. The Battle at Betar Was Lost (133 CE)
- Jews rebelled against Roman rule
- Leader Simon Bar Kochba believed to be Messiah by many
- Final fortress: Betar, southwest of Jerusalem
- Hundreds of thousands of Jews massacred
- Bar Kochba's rebellion crushed
- Date: 9th of Av

### 5. The Romans Plowed the Temple Mount (134 CE)
- One year after conquest of Betar
- Romans plowed over the Temple Mount (holiest site)
- Date: 9th of Av

### 6. Jews Expelled from England (1290 CE)
- King Edward I expelled all Jews
- Date: 9th of Av

### 7. Jews Banished from Spain (1492 CE)
- Golden Age of Spain ended
- Queen Isabella and Ferdinand ordered expulsion
- Edict signed March 31, 1492
- Four months to leave
- Final date to leave: 9th of Av
- Ended centuries of Jewish life in Spain

### Bonus: World War I Began (1914)
- Germany declared war on Russia on 9th of Av
- Catapulted WWI into motion
- Historians: WWI led directly to WWII and Holocaust

## Theological Message:
"Jews see this as another confirmation of the deeply held conviction that history isn't haphazard; events – even terrible ones – are part of a Divine plan and have spiritual meaning. The message of time is that everything has a rational purpose, even though we don't understand it."
