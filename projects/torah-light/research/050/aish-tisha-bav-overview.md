# Aish.com - What Are Tisha B'Av & the Three Weeks

**Source:** https://aish.com/what-are-tisha-bav-the-three-weeks/
**Author:** Rabbi Shraga Simmons

## Key Points:

### The Three Weeks (17th Tammuz - 9th Av)
- Period "within the straits" (bein hametzarim)
- Both First and Second Temples destroyed during this time
- Days of misfortune and calamity for Jewish people
- Minimize joy: no weddings, no music, no haircuts/shaving
- Avoid potentially dangerous activities (Divine judgment more acute)
- Shabbat Haftorahs from Isaiah/Jeremiah about Temple destruction and exile

### Purpose of Mourning
- Agonizing helps conquer spiritual deficiencies that caused tragedies
- Through teshuva (self-introspection and commitment to improve), power to transform tragedy into joy
- Talmud: After future redemption and Temple rebuilding, these days will become days of rejoicing

### Napoleon Story
Napoleon heard crying in Paris synagogue on Tisha B'Av. Asked what happened. Told Jews mourning loss of Temple. "When?" "About 1700 years ago." Napoleon: "Certainly a people which has mourned the loss of their Temple for so long, will merit to see it rebuilt!"

### 17th of Tammuz
Five catastrophes:
1. Moses broke tablets (response to Golden Calf sin)
2. Daily offerings suspended in First Temple (siege of Jerusalem)
3. Jerusalem's walls breached (before Second Temple destruction)
4. Roman general Apostamos burned Torah scroll
5. Idolatrous image placed in Temple Sanctuary

### Nine Days (from Rosh Chodesh Av)
Stricter mourning:
- No meat or wine (except Shabbat)
- No laundry or freshly laundered clothes (except Shabbat)
- No bathing for pleasure (cool water to remove dirt/sweat ok)
- Talmudic dictum: "When month of Av begins, we reduce our joy"

### Tisha B'Av – Five National Calamities:
1. Jews in desert accepted slanderous report of 12 Spies
2. First Temple destroyed
3. Second Temple destroyed
4. Bar Kochba revolt crushed
5. Temple Mount plowed, Jerusalem rebuilt as pagan city
+ Expulsion from Spain 1492

### Tisha B'Av Practices:
- Seudah Hamaf-seket (final meal before fast)
- No eating/drinking from sundown to nightfall next evening
- Forbidden: bathing/washing, leather shoes, marital relations
- No Torah study except texts about mourning (Lamentations, Job, certain Talmud sections like Kamtza and Bar Kamtza story)
- Book of Eicha (Lamentations) read
- Sit on low chair (after midday, regular chair permitted)
- Some practices delayed until midday 10th of Av (burning of Temple continued through 10th)
