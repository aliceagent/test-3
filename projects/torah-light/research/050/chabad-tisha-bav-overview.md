# Chabad.org - Three Weeks and Tisha B'Av Overview

**Source:** https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/144573/jewish/What-Is-Tisha-BAv.htm

## Key Points:

### The Three Weeks: What and Why
- Period "between the strictures" from Tammuz 17 to Av 9
- Both First and Second Temples destroyed during this period
- Many calamities befell Jewish people throughout generations
- 830 years the Temple stood in Jerusalem

### Conduct During the 3 Weeks
- No weddings (engagement parties without music allowed until Rosh Chodesh Av)
- No playing musical instruments or listening to music
- No reciting Shehecheyanu blessing (on new items)
- No haircuts or shaving (many Sephardic Jews only from Rosh Chodesh Av)

### Shabbat During the Three Weeks
- All mourning suspended on Shabbat
- Shabbat constitutes foretaste of Messianic Era
- Only focus on positive elements

### Determination to Rebuild
- Rebbe urged increased Torah study and charity during Three Weeks
- Midrash: Study of Temple design equals actual construction
- Holy Temple section recommended for detailed study

### From Destruction to Renewal
- Prophet describes fasts as "days of goodwill before G-d"
- Days of opportunity to exploit failings as impetus for renewed bond with G-d
- Purification accompanies fasting
- Promise of redemption pervades the mourning
- Current of joy underlies the sadness
- Ninth of Av is birthday of Moshiach

### Prophecy Quote
"I will turn their mourning into joy and will comfort them and make them rejoice from their sorrow" (Jeremiah 31:12)
