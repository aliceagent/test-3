# Aish.com - Mezuzah: Prayer, Placement & Meaning

Source: https://aish.com/mezuzah-prayer-placement-meaning/

## Key Content Summary:

### Definition and Meaning
- A Mezuzah is a small scroll inscribed with Torah verses, usually placed in a case, and fixed to the doorpost of a Jewish home
- The mezuzah recalls the Exodus from Egypt, when lamb's blood on doorposts identified Jewish homes during the plague of the firstborn
- It's a daily reminder and public declaration of Jewish identity and faith

### Biblical Basis
- Contains first two paragraphs of the "Shema" prayer (Deuteronomy 6:4-9 and 11:13-21)
- Commands to "write [these words] on the doorpost of your house and on your gates"
- The second passage teaches that Jewish destiny depends on fulfilling God's will

### Spiritual Significance
- Distinguishes human dwelling (dirah) from animal dwelling (dir) - the difference is the letter hey (Name of God)
- Protects against "evil spirits" - not supernatural ones, but negative influences we can control
- On reverse side: Hebrew name Shaddai ("Guardian of the Doors of Israel")

### Kosher Requirements
- Must be hand-written on genuine parchment from kosher animal
- Written by specially trained scribe (sofer) with special black ink and quill
- Any mistakes or missing letters invalidate the entire parchment
- Should cost $30-40 from certified sources
- Should be checked twice every seven years for cracks/fading

### Placement Rules
- Right-hand doorpost as you enter
- Lower part of upper-third of doorpost (shoulder height)
- Ashkenazi custom: slight angle with top pointing toward room
- Sephardi custom: straight up vertically
- Must be permanently affixed (glue, nails, screws - not tape/velcro)
- Required on most doors people use (not bathrooms/small closets)

### Blessing and Installation
**Hebrew:** בָּרוּך אַתָּה אַדָנָ-י אֶלוֹהֵ-ינוּ מֶלֶך הָעוֹלָם אַשֶר קְדִשָנוּ בְּמִצְווֹתָיו וְצִיווָנוּ לִקְבּוֹעַ מְזוּזָה

**Translation:** "Blessed are you, L-rd our G‑d, King of the Universe, Who has made us holy with His commandments and commanded us to affix a mezuzah."

### Moving Considerations
- New home: put up within 30 days (diaspora) or immediately (Israel)
- Don't remove if next occupants are Jewish
- New occupants should pay for existing mezuzot

### Daily Practice
- Many touch mezuzah when passing through doorway and kiss hand
- Serves as reminder to focus on God's instructions
- Original "Jewish billboard" with eternal message
