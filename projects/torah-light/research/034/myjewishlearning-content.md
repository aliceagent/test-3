# My Jewish Learning - Why Jews Hang a Mezuzah on the Doorpost

Source: https://www.myjewishlearning.com/article/mezuzah/

## Key Content Summary:

### Core Concept
- "A mezuzah declares: The people who dwell here live Jewish lives"
- Creates sanctified permanent residence through concrete ritual
- Judaism generally oriented to holiness of time, but mezuzah sanctifies place

### Biblical Origins
- Of biblical origin: "And you shall inscribe them on the doorposts (mezuzot) of our house and on your gates" (Deuteronomy 6:9, 11:20)
- What to inscribe: "The words that I shall tell you this day" - love God, keep commandments, teach children

### Two Functions
1. Reminder: every entry/exit reminds of covenant with God
2. Symbol: tells everyone this is a Jewish household with special rules, rituals, beliefs

### The Scroll (Klaf)
- Must be hand-lettered by kosher scribe observant of halacha
- Case has no special requirements (any size/shape/material)
- Scroll rolled left to right so first words appear first
- Opened twice in seven years for inspection

### Placement Laws
- Every living space with two doorposts and overhead lintel
- NOT: bathrooms, closets, laundry room, boiler room
- Right side of door as you enter
- Lower part of top third of doorpost (eyeball height)
- Affixed at slant, lower part toward you
- Must be secured top and bottom (not hanging from nail)

### Timing Rules
- Up to 30 days after moving (diaspora)
- Immediately when moving (Israel)
- Temporary residence (less than 30 days): no mezuzah needed
- Dormitory: needs mezuzah if considered "home away from home"

### Moving Considerations
- Don't remove if another Jewish family moving in
- Remove if non-Jewish family following (lest they be thrown away)
- Can substitute valuable case but klaf should remain

### Blessing Ritual
**Hebrew:** בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְהֹוָה , אֱלֹהֵֽינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם, אֲשֶׁר קִדְּשָׁנוּ בְּמִצְוֹתָיו וְצִוָּנוּ לִקְבֹּעַ מְזוּזָה

**Transliteration:** Barukh ata Adonai Eloheinu melekh ha-olam, asher kiddeshanu be-mitzvotav ve-tzivvanu likboa mezuzah

**Translation:** "Blessed are You, Lord our God, Ruler of the universe, who has sanctified us with His commandments and commanded us to affix a mezuzah"

### Not an Amulet
- Reverse side shows "Shaddai" (Guardian of doors of Israel)
- Some mistakenly treat as protective charm/amulet
- True purpose: sign and reminder of Covenant, love, commitment
- Creates Jewish household - that itself is sufficient
