# Sefaria - Biblical Sources for Mezuzah

## Deuteronomy 6:4-9 (First Paragraph of Mezuzah)

**The Shema:**
"Hear, O Israel! The ETERNAL is our God, the ETERNAL alone. You shall love the ETERNAL your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your might. Take to heart these instructions with which I charge you this day. Impress them upon your children. Recite them when you stay at home and when you are away, when you lie down and when you get up. Bind them as a sign on your hand and let them serve as a symbol on your forehead; inscribe them on the doorposts of your house and on your gates."

## Deuteronomy 11:13-21 (Second Paragraph of Mezuzah)

**Key verses about rewards and consequences:**
"If, then, you obey the commandments that I enjoin upon you this day, loving the ETERNAL your God and rendering service with all your heart and soul, I will grant the rain for your land in season, the early rain and the late. You shall gather in your new grain and wine and oil— I will also provide grass in the fields for your cattle—and thus you shall eat your fill. Take care not to be lured away to serve other gods and bow to them. For GOD's anger will flare up against you, shutting up the skies so that there will be no rain and the ground will not yield its produce; and you will soon perish from the good land that GOD is assigning to you.

Therefore impress these My words upon your very heart: bind them as a sign on your hand and let them serve as a symbol on your forehead, and teach them to your children—reciting them when you stay at home and when you are away, when you lie down and when you get up; and inscribe them on the doorposts of your house and on your gates— to the end that you and your children may endure, in the land that GOD swore to your fathers to assign to them, as long as there is a heaven over the earth."

## Key Commandments in These Passages:

1. **Love God** with all heart, soul, and might
2. **Take to heart** these instructions
3. **Teach children** - impress upon them
4. **Recite regularly** - when home/away, lying down/getting up
5. **Bind on hand** and forehead (tefillin)
6. **Inscribe on doorposts** and gates (mezuzah)

## Historical Context:
These passages are part of Moses's final speeches to the Israelites before entering the Promised Land (during the late Shang Dynasty period in Chinese chronology, approximately 1300-1260 BCE). They form the core of Jewish daily prayer (Shema) and are the actual text written inside every mezuzah scroll.

## Spiritual Themes:
- Unity and uniqueness of God
- Total love and devotion required
- Constant awareness and remembrance
- Teaching the next generation
- Physical symbols as spiritual reminders
- Consequences of obedience vs. disobedience
- Longevity in the land through faithfulness
