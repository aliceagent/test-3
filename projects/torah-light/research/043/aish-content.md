# Aish.com: Building a Sukkah

**Source 1:** https://aish.com/48960921/  
**Source 2:** https://aish.com/48969031/

## Basic Requirements

### Site Selection
- Select a site that has nothing hanging above it (no roof or tree)
- The sukkah floor space must be at least 27 inches by 27 inches (67 cm) - minimum space for most of a person to sit with a small table
- An apartment balcony will work fine provided it has no roof

### Walls
- A "kosher" sukkah needs at least two complete walls and a small part of a third wall
- The walls can be of any material, as long as they are sturdy enough to withstand a normal wind
- The walls should be at least 38 inches high (96 cm), but not higher than 30 feet (9.6 m)
- You don't have to build walls especially for the sukkah; you can use the side of a building, or even a hedge of bushes

### Roof (S'chach)
- Must be made from something that grew from the ground
- Must be detached from the ground when used
- Material can only be added AFTER the walls are built
- The roof must be sufficiently covered so that it gives more shade than sun during the daytime
- Yet it should be sufficiently open so that the stars are visible through the roof at night

## Detailed Halachic Requirements

### When to Build
- According to most opinions, one should begin at the departure of Yom Kippur
- If not possible, begin the following morning
- If insufficient time to complete, one may begin to build before Yom Kippur, but should not place the s'chach until after Yom Kippur
- Ideally, the sukkah should be completed on the day after Yom Kippur

### Who Can Build
- Any person may build a sukkah, including women and children
- It is a mitzvah for every man to participate personally in the building
- Whoever toils and sweats with this task receives atonement for serious sins
- It is preferable not to ask a gentile to build a sukkah

### Location Requirements
- Must be built under the sky, with nothing intervening between the s'chach and the sky
- Cannot build under: a roof, an overhanging balcony, a tree, protruding s'chach of another sukkah

### Size Requirements
**Minimum Size:**
- Inside must measure at least seven tefachim long by seven tefachim wide (70cm x 70cm preferred, 56cm x 56cm still kosher)
- Internal height should be at least 1m, but still kosher if only 80cm

**Maximum Size:**
- No limit to size
- S'chach must not be higher than 20 amot from the floor (approx. 10 meters)

### Wall Materials and Construction
- Walls may be made from any sturdy material
- Must be strong enough to remain in position when the wind blows
- Sheets that flap may not be used
- If no alternatives available, may use sheets tied down on all sides
- The walls must be at least 80cm high (preferably 1m) but do not need to reach the s'chach

### S'chach Materials
Must satisfy three conditions:
1. It is vegetation
2. It is detached from the ground
3. It is incapable of becoming tamei (ritually impure)

**Best s'chach:** Cut branches of trees
**Cannot use:** Wide planks wider than 8cm (preferably not wider than 5cm)

### S'chach Coverage
- Must use sufficient s'chach to cover the majority of the roof area
- Ideally, there should be sufficient spaces that one will be able to see some stars at night
- If no spaces at all, the sukkah is still kosher
- Must reach within 24cm of the walls

### Construction Order
- The walls must be constructed BEFORE the s'chach is put in place
- If s'chach is placed first, the sukkah is invalid and the s'chach should be raised and lowered

### Decoration
- Since the sukkah is designated as your "home" for seven days, it is customary to decorate it nicely
- Many people hang fruits and flowers from the ceiling
- Tape posters of Jerusalem and other Jewish themes on the walls
