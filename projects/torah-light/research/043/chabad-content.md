# Chabad.org: How to Build a Sukkah

**Source:** https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/420823/jewish/How-to-Build-a-Sukkah.htm

## Key Points

### Where to Build
- Construct your sukkah outdoors, ideally in a spot that's most accessible to your residence
- Popular sukkah locations include: porches, backyards, courtyards, lawns, balconies and rooftops
- Basically, any location under the open sky
- Important requirement: nothing between your sukkah and the open sky (no trees, canopies or roofs overhanging)

### Materials Needed

**The Walls:**
- Can be made from any sturdy material
- Prefabricated sukkahs available from many Judaica vendors with rabbinical certification

**The Roof Covering (Sechach):**
- Must be made of vegetable matter that has been cut from the ground
- Cannot be processed or manufactured into anything else
- Examples: bamboo poles, corn stalks, or evergreen branches
- Mats made of bamboo, straw or other vegetable matter can be used only if they were made for the purpose of serving as a roof covering

### Dimensions and Requirements

**The Walls:**
- A sukkah must have at least two full walls plus part of a third wall (the "part" needs to be a minimum of 3.2 inches wide)
- It is preferable that the sukkah have four complete walls
- The walls must be at least 32 inches high, and the entire structure may not be taller than 30 feet
- In length and breadth, a sukkah cannot be smaller than 22.4 inches by 22.4 inches
- There is no size limit in how large—in length and width—a sukkah may be

**The Sechach:**
- There must be sufficient sechach to provide enough shade so that in a bright midday there is more shade than sun seen on the floor of the sukkah
- Yet there should be gaps in the sechach so you can see the stars at night
- Anything that is directly supporting the sechach must also be made of materials that are derived from and remain connected to the ground

### Construction Details
- A sukkah must be built anew every year for the purpose of the mitzvah (this requirement applies only to the sechach)
- One must first erect the walls and only then place the sechach
- It is best that a sukkah have four solid walls
- There may be gaps of empty space in the walls, as long as these are less than 9.6 inches wide

### Lighting and Furnishing
- Electric lights can be strung (use weatherproof outdoor lighting)
- Chairs and tables needed for dining
- Customary to decorate nicely - many people hang fruits and flowers from the ceiling, and tape posters of Jerusalem and other Jewish themes on the walls
