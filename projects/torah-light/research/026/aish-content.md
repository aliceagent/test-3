# Aish.com Content - Kosher Symbols

Source: https://aish.com/kosher-symbols/

## Key Points from Aish.com

### The "K" Problem
- A plain "K" on packaging does NOT guarantee kosher certification
- In America, there is NO LAW preventing companies from putting any letter on labels
- Companies can continue using "K" even after losing kosher certification

### Real-Life Example:
A rabbi supervising a kosher bakery in Florida told them he would remove certification if they used a non-kosher ingredient. The bakery's response: "That's okay, we'll just continue putting a 'K' on our packaging anyway."

### Reliable Hechshers (Certification Symbols):
The most common and reliable symbols include:
- **OU** (Orthodox Union)
- **OK** (OK Kosher Certification)
- **Star-K**
- **Chaf-K** (Kof-K)

### Important Warning:
- Many symbols exist - "some good and some not-so-good"
- When in doubt, check with a rabbi well-versed in Jewish law
- Reference website: www.kosherquest.org

### Trademark Protection:
- Reputable hechshers are registered trademarks
- Cannot be used without permission from certifying authority
- But plain "K" or made-up symbols are not protected by law
