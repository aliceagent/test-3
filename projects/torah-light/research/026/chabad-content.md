# Chabad.org Content - Kosher Certification

Source: https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/113425/jewish/Kosher-Symbols.htm

## Key Points from Chabad.org

### What is Kosher Certification?
- Processed foods and establishments require certification by a reliable rabbi or kashrut supervision agency
- A copyrighted kashrut symbol (hechsher) indicates kosher certification
- The intricacies of modern food technology make it impossible for non-experts to verify if processed food is kosher

### Why Certification is Needed
- Even a small trace of non-kosher substance (as little as 1/60th or 1.66% of food's volume) can render food non-kosher
- Utensils absorb "taste" from hot foods and can impart it to other foods
- Modern food processing uses shared equipment that can contaminate products

### Examples of Hidden Non-Kosher Issues:
1. Bread baked in a pan with lard-containing shortening
2. Fruit juice pasteurized in same machinery as non-kosher milk
3. Vegetarian dish prepared with utensils used for non-kosher food

### Separate Kitchen Requirements
- All cooking utensils must be used exclusively for kosher food
- Separate equipment needed for meat and dairy:
  - Stoves
  - Pots
  - Cutlery
  - Dishes
  - Counter surfaces
  - Table coverings

### The Role of Experts
- Requires expert knowledge of food technology
- Only qualified rabbis or agencies can properly certify
- Look for copyrighted kashrut symbols on packaging
