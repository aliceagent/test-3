2. We Count Days and Weeks
On the first day, we say, “Today is one day to
the Omer.” Once we reach the seventh day, we count weeks as well, saying,
“Today is seven days, which is one week to the Omer.” On the following day, we
say, “Today is eight days, which is one week and one day to the Omer.”

7. We Count at Night
The best time to count the Omer is after
evening services, right after the new Jewish day (which starts at nightfall)
has begun, so that you have a full day ahead of you. In the event that you
forgot to count at night, you can count the following day as well, but you do
not say the customary blessing.
Read: 
Why Do We Count the Omer at Night?
8. Every Day Counts
Omer is a unique mitzvah in that it spans 49
days. In the event that you missed a day entirely, you should continue to count
each night just as before, but you do not say the customary blessing, since it
is possible that the missed day invalidated the completion of your mitzvah. 
Read: 
I Keep Forgetting to Count the Omer!
9. The Sadducees Counted Differently
Scripture tells us to begin counting the Omer
“on the day after the rest day.” Tradition understands this to mean the day
after the (first day of) Passover. However, there were once Jewish splinter
groups that interpreted it to mean that the count must always begin on a
