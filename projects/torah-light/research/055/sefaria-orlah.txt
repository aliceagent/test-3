=== SEFARIA: Leviticus 19:23-25 ===
Source: https://www.sefaria.org/api/texts/Leviticus%2019%3A23-25
Hebrew Title: ויקרא
English Title: N/A

1. G<small>OD</small> spoke to Moses, saying: 

2. Speak to the whole Israelite community and say to them:<br>You shall be holy, for I, the E<small>TERNAL</small> your God, am holy.

3. You shall each revere your mother and your father, and keep My sabbaths: I the E<small>TERNAL</small> am your God.

4. Do not turn to idols or make molten gods for yourselves: I the E<small>TERNAL</small> am your God.

5. When you sacrifice an offering of well-being to G<small>OD</small>, sacrifice it so that it may be accepted in your behalf. 

6. It shall be eaten on the day you sacrifice it, or on the day after; but what is left by the third day must be consumed in fire. 

7. If it should be eaten on the third day, it is an offensive thing, it will not be acceptable. 

8. And one who eats of it shall bear the guilt for having profaned what is sacred to G<small>OD</small>; that person shall be cut off from kin.

9. When you reap the harvest of your land, you shall not reap all the way to the edges of your field, or gather the gleanings of your harvest. 

10. You shall not pick your vineyard bare, or gather the fallen fruit of your vineyard; you shall leave them for the poor and the stranger: I the E<small>TERNAL</small> am your God.

11. You shall not steal; you shall not deal deceitfully or falsely with one another.

12. You shall not swear falsely by My name, profaning the name of your God: I am the E<small>TERNAL</small>.

13. You shall not defraud your fellow.<sup class="footnote-marker">a</sup><i class="footnote"><b>fellow </b>I.e., fellow Israelite—whether male or female. In contrast to others “neighbor.”</i> You shall not commit robbery. The wages of a laborer shall not remain with you until morning.

14. You shall not insult the deaf, or place a stumbling block before the blind. You shall fear your God: I am the E<small>TERNAL</small>.

15. You shall not render an unfair decision: do not favor the poor or show deference to the rich; judge your kindred fairly.

16. Do not deal basely with<sup class="footnote-marker">b</sup><i class="footnote"><b>deal basely with </b>E.g., via slander; in contrast to others “go about as a talebearer among”; meaning of Heb. idiom <i>halakh rakhil be-</i> uncertain.</i> members of your people. Do not profit by<sup class="footnote-marker">c</sup><i class="footnote"><b>profit by </b>Lit. “stand upon”; precise meaning of Heb. idiom <i>ʻamad ʻal</i> uncertain.</i> the blood of your fellow:<sup class="footnote-marker">d</sup><i class="footnote"><b>fellow </b>See note at v. <a class="refLink" data-ref="Leviticus 19:13" href="Leviticus.19.13">13</a>.</i> I am G<small>OD</small>.

17. You shall not hate your kinsfolk in your heart. Reprove your kindred but<sup class="footnote-marker">e</sup><i class="footnote"><b>but </b>Exact force of <i>we-</i> uncertain.</i> incur no guilt on their account. 

18. You shall not take vengeance or bear a grudge against members of your people. Love your fellow<sup class="footnote-marker">f</sup><i class="footnote"><b>fellow </b>See note at v. <a class="refLink" data-ref="Leviticus 19:13" href="Leviticus.19.13">13</a>, and cf. v. <a class="refLink" data-ref="Leviticus 19:34" href="Leviticus.19.34">34</a>.</i> as yourself: I am G<small>OD</small>.

19. You shall observe My laws.<br>You shall not let your cattle mate with a different kind; you shall not sow your field with two kinds of seed; you shall not put on cloth from a mixture of two kinds of material.

20. If a man has carnal relations with a woman who is a slave and has been designated for another man, but has not been redeemed or given her freedom, there shall be an indemnity; they shall not, however, be put to death, since she has not been freed. 

21. But he must bring to the entrance of the Tent of Meeting, as his reparation offering to G<small>OD</small>, a ram of reparation offering. 

22. With the ram of reparation offering the priest shall make expiation for him before G<small>OD</small> for the sin that he committed; and the sin that he committed will be forgiven him.

23. When you enter the land and plant any tree for food, you shall regard its fruit as forbidden. Three years it shall be forbidden<sup class="footnote-marker">g</sup><i class="footnote"><b>forbidden </b>Heb. root <i>ʻ-r-l</i>, commonly “to be uncircumcised.”</i> for you, not to be eaten. 

24. In the fourth year all its fruit shall be set aside for jubilation before G<small>OD</small>;

25. and only in the fifth year may you use its fruit—that its yield to you may be increased: I the E<small>TERNAL</small> am your God.

26. You shall not eat anything with its blood. You shall not practice divination or soothsaying.

27. You [men] shall not round off the side-growth on your head, or destroy the side-growth of your beard. 

28. You shall not make gashes in your flesh for the dead, or incise any marks on yourselves: I am G<small>OD</small>.

29. Do not degrade your daughter and make her a prostitute, lest the land fall into prostitution and the land be filled with depravity. 

30. You shall keep My sabbaths and venerate My sanctuary: I am G<small>OD</small>.

31. Do not turn to ghosts and do not inquire of familiar spirits, to be defiled by them: I the E<small>TERNAL</small> am your God.

32. You shall rise before the aged and show deference to the old; you shall fear your God: I am the E<small>TERNAL</small>.

33. When strangers reside with you in your land, you shall not wrong them. 

34. The strangers who reside with you shall be to you as your citizens; you shall love each one as yourself, for you were strangers in the land of Egypt: I the E<small>TERNAL</small> am your God.

35. You shall not falsify measures of length, weight, or capacity. 

36. You shall have an honest balance, honest weights, an honest <i>ephah</i>, and an honest <i>hin</i>.<br>I the E<small>TERNAL</small> am your God who freed you from the land of Egypt. 

37. You shall faithfully observe all My laws and all My rules: I am G<small>OD</small>.
