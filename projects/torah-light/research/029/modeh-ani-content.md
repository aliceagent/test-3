# Modeh Ani - The First Prayer of the Day

## Basic Information
- **Hebrew**: מודה אני (male), מודה אני (female)
- **Transliteration**: Modeh ani (male), Modah ani (female)
- **Meaning**: "I give thanks"
- **When**: Immediately upon waking, while still in bed
- **Unique feature**: Contains no divine names, can be said before washing hands

## Full Text

### Hebrew:
מוֹדֶה אֲנִי לְפָנֶֽיךָ מֶֽלֶךְ חַי וְקַיָּים. שֶׁהֶֽחֱזַֽרְתָּ בִּי נִשְׁמָתִי ,בְּחֶמְלָה. רַבָּה אֱמֽוּנָתֶֽךָ׃

### Transliteration:
Modeh ani lefanekha melekh chai vekayam sheheḥezarta bi nishmati b'ḥemlah, rabah emunatekha.

### English Translation:
I give thanks before you, King living and eternal, for You have returned within me my soul with compassion; abundant is Your faithfulness.

## Historical Context
- First appears in 16th century work "Seder haYom" by Rabbi Moshe ben Machir
- Not mentioned in Talmud or Shulchan Aruch
- Based on Lamentations 3:22-23: "The Lord's mercies are not consumed, surely His compassions do not fail. They are new every morning; great is Your faithfulness"
- Replaces older practice of saying "Elohai Neshamah" immediately upon waking

## Spiritual Significance
- Expresses gratitude for waking up alive
- Recognizes that sleep is like a mini-death and waking is rebirth
- First conscious act is gratitude to God
- Sets tone of thankfulness for entire day
- Acknowledges that life itself is a gift

## Practical Guide
1. Say immediately upon waking, before getting out of bed
2. No need to wash hands first (contains no divine names)
3. Can be said quietly or silently
4. Pause slightly between "b'chemlah" (with compassion) and "rabah" (abundant)
5. Think about the meaning while reciting

## Chinese Cultural Parallels
- Similar to Chinese practice of morning reflection and gratitude
- Connects to Confucian emphasis on recognizing benefits received
- Parallels Chinese concept of "天道" (Tiandao) - the Way of Heaven's providence
- Like Chinese ancestor veneration, acknowledges source of life and blessing
