# Modeh Ani Teaching Sheet - Sefaria

Source: https://voices.sefaria.org/sheets/416419

## Prayer Text and Analysis

**Hebrew**: (א) מוֹדֶה אֲנִי לְפָנֶֽיךָ מֶֽלֶךְ חַי וְקַיָּם שֶׁהֶחֱזַֽרְתָּ בִּי נִשְׁמָתִי בְּחֶמְלָה, רַבָּה אֱמוּנָתֶֽךָ:

**Translation**: I give thanks to You living and everlasting King for You have restored my soul with mercy. Great is Your faithfulness.

### Word Order Significance

**Question**: Why does it say "Thankful am I" and not "I am thankful"? Wouldn't that make more sense? Why are the words switched?

**Answer**: The very first word that comes out of our mouths should be one of gratitude. We often spend the rest of our waking day focused on ourselves, our needs, our work. It is important to ensure that we never forget to have the foundation of our day be "Thank You", acknowledging that whatever else comes in our day is from G-d. Only after that has been said do we mention ourselves.

**Principle**: We do not want the first word of our mouth in the morning focused on 'I', but rather shifting our perspective to one of gratitude and first saying 'thank you'.

## Medical/Scientific Connection

### The 12-Second Phenomenon

**Medical research**: During a recent neurological conference, neurologists discussed people who faint as soon as they get out of bed in the morning.

**Professor Linda McMahon's findings**: After years of study, she concluded that the fainting phenomenon was the result of an overly quick transition between the prone and standing positions.

**The solution**: The blood in the body requires twelve seconds for blood to flow from the feet to the brain. She recommended that every person waking up should sit up on their bed and slowly count to twelve.

**Jewish connection**: A religious Jewish professor pointed out: "We Jews hold by an old tradition to recite a prayer every morning... This prayer is recited immediately upon opening our eyes, while sitting on our bed. This prayer is composed of twelve words, and if we recite it slowly, with concentration, it takes exactly twelve seconds to say."

## Personal Testimony - The Lubavitcher Rebbe's Perspective

### The Rebbe's Favorite Prayer

**Story**: The wife of the Lubavitcher Rebbe was asked by a young boy what the Rebbe's favorite prayer was.

**Her response**: "It's a very short prayer. It's the very first prayer we say in the morning, Modeh Ani..."

**The Rebbe's interpretation**: The message of this prayer is that G-d has a lot of faith in us, so let's not disappoint Him. How do we know that the Creator has faith in us? Because He gave us another day of life, even though it is not owed to us.

**Life goal**: It became the author's life goal - not to disappoint G-d in what is accomplished during each day.

### Daily Purpose and Calling

**Core message**: Every time we wake up we are gifted with another day, Hashem is calling to you saying that you specifically are needed in this world for a purpose only you can do.

**Daily reflection**: In the morning, try and think about what that could be and what bit of good you can bring to the world, one day at a time.

## How Should We Wake Up?

**Shulchan Aruch instruction**: The first instruction in the Code of Jewish Law is: "Be strong as a lion when you wake-up in the morning to serve your Creator."

**Interpretation**: The way in which a person gets up in the morning indicates their general emotional state, and influences one's actions throughout the day.

- **Person with purpose**: Arises filled with enthusiasm to face a new day. Usually wakes up early to accomplish more throughout the day.
- **Person without purpose**: Lacks meaning in life and a reason to get up. May feel fatigued and distressed in the morning, waking up late and sluggish.

**Rebbe Nachman's teaching**: "The day you were born is the day Hashem decided that the world could no longer continue without you."
