# Modeh Ani - Wikipedia Content

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modeh_Ani

## Key Information

**Modeh Ani** (Hebrew: מודה אני; "I give thanks") is a Jewish prayer that observant Jews recite daily upon waking, while still in bed.

### Prayer Text

**Male version:**
Hebrew: מוֹדֶה אֲנִי לְפָנֶֽיךָ מֶֽלֶךְ חַי וְקַיָּים. שֶׁהֶֽחֱזַֽרְתָּ בִּי נִשְׁמָתִי ,בְּחֶמְלָה. רַבָּה אֱמֽוּנָתֶֽךָ׃

Transliteration: Modeh ani lefanekha melekh cḥai vekayam sheheḥezarta bi nishmati b'ḥemlah, rabah emunatekha.

English: I give thanks before you, King living and eternal, for You have returned within me my soul with compassion; abundant is Your faithfulness.

**Female version:**
Hebrew: מוֹדָה אֲנִי לְפָנֶֽיךָ מֶֽלֶךְ חַי וְקַיָּים. שֶׁהֶֽחֱזַֽרְתָּ בִּי נִשְׁמָתִי ,בְּחֶמְלָה. רַבָּה אֱמֽוּנָתֶֽךָ׃

Transliteration: Modah ani lefanekha melekh cḥai vekayam sheheḥezarta bi nishmati b'ḥemlah, rabah emunatekha.

### Historical Background

- **Biblical basis**: Lamentations states that "The Lord's mercies are not consumed, surely His compassions do not fail. They are new every morning; great is Your faithfulness."
- **Talmudic teaching**: From this, the Shulchan Aruch deduces that every morning, God renews every person as a new creation.
- **Historical origin**: The specific prayer Modeh Ani is not mentioned in the Talmud or Shulchan Aruch, and first appears in the work *Seder haYom* by the 16th century rabbi Moshe ben Machir.

### Practical Guidelines

- **When to recite**: Upon waking, while still in bed
- **Special feature**: Does not include any of the names of God, so observant Jews may recite it before washing their hands
- **Pronunciation note**: According to the Kitzur Shulchan Aruch, one should pause slightly between the words "compassion" and "abundant"

### Historical Context

In Talmudic times, Jews traditionally recited Elohai Neshamah (Hebrew: אֱלהַי נְשָׁמָה, "My God, the soul") upon waking. This prayer was later moved to the morning synagogue services.
