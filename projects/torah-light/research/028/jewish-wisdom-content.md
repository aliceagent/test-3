# Modeh Ani Prayer Analysis - Jewish Wisdom

Source: https://www.jewishwisdom.co/jewish-prayer/modeh-ani-jewish-prayer-thanks/

## Prayer Translation and Analysis

**Translation**: "Thank you, oh living and eternal God, for mercifully returning my soul to me, great is your faithfulness."

### Starting with Gratitude

**Key insight**: The first words of the prayer, and therefore the first words that are supposed to come out of our mouth to start our new day, are "thank you."

**Significance**: We don't start off by asking God for something. We don't cry out to him, or complain, or kvetch. Instead, we acknowledge the awesome gift of life that He has given us and say Thank You.

**Life lesson**: To see the gifts that we have been blessed with and express our gratitude to the giver of those gifts.

### Soul's Return

**Teaching**: "for mercifully returning my soul to me" - This refers to the Jewish teaching that our soul leaves our body when we sleep at night, and is returned to us in the morning.

**Talmudic reference**: The Talmud refers to sleep as 1/60 of death.

**Perspective**: When we wake up from a night's sleep, it's as if we are being reborn. We are given the opportunity to look at the world with fresh eyes and a fresh outlook.

**Lesson**: To wake up in the morning and view each day as a new opportunity to achieve our goals.

### "Great is Your Faithfulness" - God's Faith in Us

**Hebrew analysis**: The final part reads "Rabah Enumatecha" or "Great is your faith" - not "Great is your faithfulness."

**Key interpretation**: God has faith in us. God has faith in us, that we will live another day in a positive manner, by doing good deeds and following His commandments.

**Analogy**: Like a parent who lets a young child carry a tray of glasses across the room for the first time. The parent knows that the child might fail and that the glasses might all come crashing down, but he also knows that in order for the child to learn he must give the child the opportunity to succeed and show the child that he believes in his ability to succeed.

**Core message**: God believes in our potential to succeed in leading a good, kind and meaningful life.

## Three Key Lessons from Modeh Ani:

1. **Be aware of the gifts you get and say Thank You to the one giving them.**
2. **View each new day as a brand new opportunity to accomplish your goals and to make it meaningful.**
3. **God believes in our potential to succeed in leading a good, kind and meaningful life. Let's not let Him down.**
