## Main Concepts

### What is Chametz?
- Leavened bread products made from wheat, barley, rye, oats, or spelt that have fermented
- Torah prohibition: "Seven days shall you eat unleavened bread; but on the first day you shall remove leaven from your houses" (Exodus 12:15)
- Includes bread, crackers, pasta, cookies, beer, cereal, etc.
- Even tiny amounts are prohibited during Passover

### Why Clean for Pesach?
- Biblical commandment to remove all chametz before Passover
- Not just about physical cleanliness - it's about removing spiritual "leaven" (ego, arrogance)
- Prepares the home to become like a mini-Temple

### The Cleaning Process

#### When to Start
- Traditionally begins 30 days before Passover
- Intensive cleaning in the final 2 weeks
- Final search (bedikat chametz) on the night before Passover eve

#### What Needs to be Cleaned
**Must clean:**
- Kitchen (all surfaces, appliances, utensils)
- Dining room (table, chairs)
- Living room (sofas, carpets)
- Bedrooms where food is eaten
- Car (if you eat in it)
- Pockets of clothing
- Children's play areas

**Don't need to clean:**
- Storage rooms not entered with food
- Furniture that hasn't moved all year and won't during Passover
- Areas at least 10 inches off the ground that aren't accessed
- Cracks and crevices where chametz is less than olive-sized

#### Room-by-Room Guide

**Kitchen:**
- Clean all cabinets and drawers
- Scrub countertops, sinks, stovetop
- Clean inside oven, microwave, refrigerator, dishwasher
- Either kasher utensils or use separate Passover dishes
- Cover surfaces that can't be kashered

**Dining Room:**
- Vacuum under table and chairs thoroughly  
- Check chair cushions and upholstery
- Wipe down table legs and chair legs

**Living Room:**
- Vacuum sofas and armchairs (especially cushions)
- Check between couch cushions
- Clean coffee tables
- Vacuum carpets thoroughly

**Bedrooms:**
- Check nightstands
- Clean under beds
- Check pockets of all clothing
- Clean children's toy boxes

**Bathrooms:**
- Usually don't need deep cleaning (no food)
- Check medicine cabinets for chametz products

**Car:**
- Vacuum entire interior
- Check under seats
- Clean trunk if food stored there
- Wipe down dashboard

### Bedikat Chametz - The Search

**When:** Night before Passover eve (or Thursday night if Passover starts Saturday night)

**What you need:**
- Candle or flashlight
- Feather
- Wooden spoon
- Paper bag

**The Ritual:**
1. Hide 10 pieces of bread around the house beforehand
2. After nightfall, recite blessing
3. Search the house by candlelight in silence
4. Collect chametz with feather, sweep into spoon
5. Wrap in bag and set aside for burning
6. Recite nullification formula

**The Blessing:**
"Baruch atah Adonai, Eloheinu melech ha'olam, asher kid'shanu b'mitzvotav v'tzivanu al bi'ur chametz"
(Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who has sanctified us with His commandments and commanded us concerning the removal of chametz)

### Burning the Chametz (Bi'ur Chametz)

**When:** Morning before Passover (before 11am usually)

**How:**
- Take the bag of chametz from the search
- Add any remaining chametz in the house
- Burn it completely
- Recite final nullification

### Selling Chametz (Mechirat Chametz)

**What:** Legal sale of remaining chametz to a non-Jew

**Why:** For chametz you can't dispose of (expensive whiskey, pantry items)

**How:**
- Authorize a rabbi to act as your agent
- Rabbi sells to non-Jew before Passover
- After Passover, non-Jew sells it back
- Done through Chabad or local rabbi

### Tips for Overwhelmed Cleaners

1. **Focus on the essentials** - Kitchen and dining areas are most important
2. **Start early** - One room per day
3. **Declutter first** - Get rid of old food and unnecessary items
4. **Use checklists** - Room-by-room tracking
5. **Get family involved** - Make it fun for kids
6. **Remember the purpose** - It's about spiritual preparation, not perfection
7. **If you miss something** - The nullification formula covers overlooked chametz

### Common Questions

**Do I need to clean every crack and crevice?**
No - only chametz pieces larger than an olive need to be removed

**What about books?**
Don't need to shake out every book unless you regularly eat while reading

**What about my car?**
Yes, if you eat in it

**What if I'm traveling for Passover?**
Still need to clean and do bedikat chametz, or sell your house's chametz

**Can I use my regular dishes?**
Either kasher them or use separate Passover dishes

## Cultural Connections

### Parallels to Chinese Culture

**Spring Cleaning Tradition:**
Similar to Chinese New Year cleaning (扫尘 sǎo chén) - sweeping away dust/bad luck
- Both occur in early spring
- Both symbolize fresh start
- Both involve thorough house cleaning
- Both have spiritual significance beyond cleanliness

**Historical Period:**
The Exodus from Egypt (~1300 BCE - late Shang Dynasty 商朝晚期)

## Spiritual Dimension

Chametz represents ego and spiritual arrogance (puffed up like risen dough)
Removing it symbolizes humility before God
The search in darkness represents seeking out hidden faults
Burning represents complete transformation

## Key Takeaways

1. Cleaning for Pesach is a biblical commandment, not just spring cleaning
2. Focus on areas where food is consumed
3. The formal search (bedikat chametz) happens the night before
4. Burning chametz completes the removal
5. Selling chametz handles items you can't dispose of
6. It's about spiritual preparation, not perfection
7. The process teaches humility and thoroughness
