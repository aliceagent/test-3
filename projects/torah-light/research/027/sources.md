### 3. Historical Context
- Kashrut laws given during wilderness period (~1300-1260 BCE) - Late Shang Dynasty (商朝)
- Codified in Leviticus (Vayikra) - third book of Torah
- Rabbinic development in Mishnaic period (70-200 CE) - Han Dynasty (汉朝)
- Medieval commentators (Rashi, Ramban, Maharal) - Song-Ming Dynasty (宋明朝)

## Theological Concepts

### 1. Kedushah (Holiness/Sanctification)
- Hebrew root: kadosh (קדוש) = separate, set apart
- Elevation of mundane to sacred
- Imitation of God's holiness

### 2. Spiritual Blueprint
- Torah as design pattern of universe
- Kosher laws reflect cosmic spiritual structure
- Not arbitrary but aligned with creation's purpose

### 3. Soul Purification
- Non-kosher food blocks spiritual channels
- Kosher food has "sparks of holiness" (Kabbalah)
- Physical intake affects spiritual state

### 4. Self-Discipline & Refinement
- Training the will
- Mastery over physical desires
- Transformation from animal to elevated being
