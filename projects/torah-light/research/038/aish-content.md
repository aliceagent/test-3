# Aish.com Research Content - Nature Blessings

## Main Article: "42. Natural Phenomena"

### Key Points:
- Judaism teaches appreciation of natural world magnificence 
- Sages ordained blessings over various natural phenomena
- General guidelines apply to all nature blessings:
  - Blessings inspire awe and wonder
  - Most have 30-day rule (no repetition within 30 days)
  - Must feel genuine awe to recite blessing

### Lightning & Thunder:
- **Lightning**: "Oseh Ma'aseh Bereshit" - "Who forms the works of Creation"
- **Thunder**: "She'kocho Oog'vuraso Malay Olam" - "Whose strength and power fill the world"
- Must recite immediately upon seeing/hearing (1-2 seconds)
- Thunder blessing is stronger than lightning blessing (shows more divine might)
- Torah was given at Sinai amidst thunder and lightning
- Talmud: "Thunder was created only in order to straighten the crookedness of the heart"

### Ocean Blessings:
- **General impressive waters**: "Oseh Ma'aseh Bereshit" 
- **Ocean specifically**: "She'asa Et Ha'yam Ha'gadol" - "who made the great ocean"
- Standing at ocean shore creates feeling of being infinitesimal and humbled

### Rainbow:
- **Blessing**: "Zocher Ha'brit" - "Who remembers the covenant, is faithful in His covenant and fulfills His word"
- References Noah's covenant - rainbow as sign God will never flood world again
- Seeing rainbow is actually negative sign (world deserves punishment but for the promise)
- Only on complete rainbow

### Mountains:
- "Oseh Ma'aseh Bereshit" on unusually shaped or beautiful mountains
- Rabbi Hirsch story: "When I come before Almighty, what will I tell Him when He asks me, 'Shimshon, did you see My Alps?'"
- Must feel genuine awe at height
- Not said from airplane where height can't be appreciated

### Timing Rules:
- Most blessings: once per 30 days maximum
- Thunder/lightning: maximum once per day (unless sky completely clears then new storm)
- Must recite immediately upon witnessing phenomenon

## "Over the Rainbow" Article

### Deep Meaning of Rainbow:
- Rainbow reveals sun's true nature through fine mist
- Metaphor: righteous person uses material world as lens to reveal God's splendor
- Noah represents this ideal - walked with God while engaged in world
- Prohibition against gazing too long: don't mistake cloud for sun (pantheism error)

### The Covenant:
- God's promise to never again destroy world by flood
- Rainbow as eternal sign of this covenant
- Seeing rainbow indicates world deserves punishment but God remembers His promise

## Thunder and Lightning Article

### Spiritual Purpose:
- Talmud (Brachot 59a): "Thunder was created only in order to straighten the crookedness of the heart"
- Wake-up call from all-powerful God
- Personal example of rabbi about to do questionable act when thunder struck

### Practical Rules:
- Must begin blessing within 1-2 seconds
- Don't need to see actual bolt - sky lighting up sufficient
- Maximum once per day except if sky clears completely then new storm
- No blessing on "heat lightning" (light without thunder)
