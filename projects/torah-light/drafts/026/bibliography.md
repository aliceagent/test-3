# Bibliography for Article 026: Understanding Kosher Certification Symbols

## Primary Sources

1. **"What Is Kosher?"** — Chabad.org  
   https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/113425/jewish/Kosher-Symbols.htm  
   *(Comprehensive overview of kosher laws, certification requirements, and the role of expert supervision in modern food production)*

2. **"Kosher Symbols"** — Aish.com  
   https://aish.com/kosher-symbols/  
   *(Guide to reliable kosher certification symbols, with critical warning about plain "K" labels that lack legal protection)*

3. **"The Kosher Symbols Clarified"** — OK Kosher Certification  
   https://www.ok.org/consumers/kosher-basics/the-kosher-symbols-clarified/  
   *(Detailed explanation of OK symbol restrictions including Pareve, Dairy, DE, CY, M, P, PY, and F designations)*

4. **"About the OU"** — OU Kosher (Orthodox Union)  
   https://oukosher.org/about-the-ou/  
   *(Official description of the world's largest kosher certifier: 1.3 million+ products, 15,000 plants, 105 countries)*

5. **"Guide to Kosher Symbols and Certifications"** — The Spruce Eats  
   https://www.thespruceeats.com/kosher-symbols-certification-2121640  
   *(Consumer guide to identifying OU, Kof-K, OK, and Star-K as the most recognized global kosher certification symbols)*

6. **"Ask the Expert: Kosher Symbols"** — My Jewish Learning  
   https://www.myjewishlearning.com/article/ask-the-expert-kosher-symbols/  
   *(Explanation of how hechsher symbols work and why each symbol represents a different certifying organization)*

7. **"Kosher Certification Agency"** — Wikipedia  
   https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kosher_certification_agency  
   *(General information on kosher certification agencies, trademark symbols, and the certification process)*

8. **"Star-K"** — Wikipedia  
   https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star-K  
   *(Information about Star-K certification, including Star-S for Passover kitniyot products)*

9. **"Kosher Symbols"** — KosherQuest.org  
   https://kosherquest.org/kosher-symbols/  
   *(Directory of approved kosher symbols maintained by Rabbi Eidlitz and the Kosher Information Bureau)*

10. **"Acceptable Kashrus Agencies"** — cRc Consumer Kosher  
    https://consumer.crckosher.org/acceptable-kashrus-agencies/  
    *(List of acceptable kosher certification agencies with consumer guidance)*
