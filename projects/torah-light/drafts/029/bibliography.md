# Bibliography for Article #29: "A Beginner's Guide to Jewish Prayer: Start With These Three"

## Primary Sources

### 1. Modeh Ani Prayer
**"Modeh Ani"** — Wikipedia  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modeh_Ani  
*Comprehensive overview of the morning gratitude prayer including Hebrew text, transliteration, English translation, and historical context. Notes that prayer first appears in 16th-century work "Seder haYom" by Rabbi Moshe ben Machir and is based on Lamentations 3:22-23.*

### 2. Shema Prayer
**"Shema - Judaism 101 (JewFAQ)"** — Rabbi Tracee Rosen  
https://www.jewfaq.org/prayers_shema  
*Detailed explanation of the three paragraphs of Shema with complete Hebrew text, transliteration, English translation, and commentary. Covers the prayer's status as one of only two prayers specifically commanded in Torah and its role as the oldest fixed daily prayer in Judaism.*

### 3. Birkat HaMazon 
**"Birkat Hamazon"** — Wikipedia  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Birkat_Hamazon  
*Complete overview of Grace After Meals including its four-blessing structure, biblical commandment from Deuteronomy 8:10, historical attribution to Moses, Joshua, David/Solomon, and Rabban Gamliel, plus laws of zimmun (group blessing).*
