## Additional Context

### Historical Period
The events of Purim occurred approximately 2,400 years ago during the Persian Empire under King Achashverosh (Ahasuerus), corresponding to China's Warring States period (戰國時代, 475-221 BCE).

### Key Concepts Referenced
- **Hester Panim** (הסתר פנים): The "hidden face" of God — divine providence working through natural events
- **Pur** (פור): Persian word for "lot," referring to the lots cast by Haman to determine the date of Jewish destruction
- **Amalek** (עמלק): Ancient enemy of the Jewish people, of whom Haman was a descendant

### Chinese Cultural Parallels Cited
- 塞翁失馬 (*Sàiwēngshīmǎ*): "The old man at the frontier loses his horse" — teaching that misfortune may turn to fortune
- 仁 (*rén*): Confucian benevolence
- 义 (*yì*): Confucian righteousness
- 慈悲 (*cíbēi*): Buddhist compassion
- Spring Festival (春節) and Mid-Autumn Festival (中秋節): Chinese festivals involving family gatherings and gift-giving
