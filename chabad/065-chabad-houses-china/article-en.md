## Introduction

If you're exploring Judaism in China — whether you're a curious local, a Jewish expatriate, a student, or someone considering conversion — you may feel isolated. Judaism is not widely known or practiced here, and finding a rabbi, a Torah class, or simply a kosher meal can seem impossible.

**This is where Chabad comes in.**

Chabad-Lubavitch is a global Jewish outreach movement known for its warm, non-judgmental approach and its willingness to meet every Jew (and interested non-Jew) exactly where they are. Across China, from the bustling streets of Shanghai to the tech hubs of Shenzhen, Chabad Houses serve as homes away from home — offering Shabbat services, holiday celebrations, kosher food, Jewish education, and most importantly, **a community**.

This article is your complete directory and guide to Chabad Houses across China, including what services they offer, how to contact them, and what to expect when you visit.

## What is a Chabad House?

Before diving into the directory, let's clarify what a Chabad House actually is.

A **Chabad House** is a Jewish community center run by a Chabad rabbi (called a *shliach*, meaning "emissary") and his family. These centers exist in over 100 countries worldwide, often in places with small or scattered Jewish populations.

### What Chabad Houses Offer:

- **Shabbat Services and Meals**: Weekly Friday night and Saturday morning services, followed by communal Shabbat meals
- **Holiday Celebrations**: Public Passover Seders, Rosh Hashanah services, Chanukah candle lightings, and more
- **Kosher Food**: Many Chabad Houses have restaurants or provide kosher meals for purchase
- **Torah Classes**: Beginner-friendly classes on Jewish texts, philosophy, and practice
- **One-on-One Learning**: Personal meetings with the rabbi to discuss Judaism, conversion, or life questions
- **Lifecycle Services**: Bar/Bat Mitzvahs, weddings, baby namings, and funerals
- **Jewish Supplies**: Tefillin, mezuzahs, Shabbat candles, prayer books, and other ritual items
- **Community**: Simply a place to connect with other Jews and feel at home

### The Chabad Philosophy

Chabad is known for its **open-door policy**. You don't need to be religious, observant, or even Jewish to visit. They welcome:

- Jews of all backgrounds and levels of observance
- People considering conversion
- Non-Jewish partners or family members of Jews
- Anyone curious about Judaism

**You will never be judged, pressured, or turned away.**

## Historical Context: Jews in China

Before listing the Chabad Houses, it's worth understanding the unique Jewish history in China.

Jews have lived in China for over a thousand years. The **Kaifeng Jewish community**, established during the Song Dynasty (宋朝, 960-1279 CE), maintained synagogues and practiced Judaism for centuries. In the 20th century, thousands of Jewish refugees found safety in **Shanghai during the Holocaust**, when most of the world closed its doors to them.

Today, China's Jewish community is small but vibrant, consisting mainly of:

1. **Foreign expatriates** (businesspeople, diplomats, students)
2. **Israeli citizens** working or traveling in China
3. **Chinese citizens** who have converted to Judaism or are exploring it
4. **Descendants of the Kaifeng Jews** reconnecting with their heritage

Chabad serves all of these groups, creating a bridge between ancient Jewish tradition and modern Chinese life.

## What to Expect When You Visit

If you've never been to a Chabad House, here's what a typical visit looks like:

### First Contact

- **Call or email ahead**: While Chabad has an open-door policy, it's respectful (and practical) to let them know you're coming, especially for Shabbat meals or holidays.
- **Introduce yourself**: Let them know your background — whether you're Jewish, considering conversion, or just curious. This helps them tailor the experience.

### Shabbat Services

- **Timing**: Friday evening services typically start before sunset (check local candle-lighting times). Saturday morning services usually begin around 9:30-10:00 AM.
- **What to Wear**: Modest, respectful clothing. Men should wear a kippah (yarmulke), which will be provided if you don't have one. Women may want to bring a scarf or hat if the community is more traditional.
- **Prayer Books**: English-Hebrew siddurim (prayer books) will be available.
- **Don't Worry if You're Lost**: Nobody expects you to know the prayers or when to stand/sit. Just follow along and observe. People are happy to help.

### Shabbat Meals

- **The Atmosphere**: Warm, family-style, and welcoming. Expect singing, words of Torah from the rabbi, and lots of questions and conversation.
- **The Food**: Typically kosher versions of Jewish classics (challah, chicken soup, gefilte fish, brisket) and sometimes Chinese-style kosher dishes.
- **No Payment on Shabbat**: Chabad does not charge for Shabbat meals, though donations are gratefully accepted (give before or after Shabbat, never during).

### Asking Questions

- **Rabbis are teachers**: Feel free to ask anything — about Judaism, Jewish law, conversion, or personal matters. Chabad rabbis are trained to answer with warmth and without judgment.
- **No Question is Too Basic**: "What is this food?" "Why do we do this?" "Can I convert?" — all fair game.

Some Chabad centers (especially Shanghai and Hong Kong) offer Torah classes in Mandarin or can connect you with Mandarin-speaking Jewish educators online.

Chabad rabbis in China are sensitive to cultural differences and often draw parallels between Jewish and Chinese values (family, education, respect for elders, etc.).

### 3. **Conversion Support**

If you're considering conversion:

- Chabad rabbis can guide you through the process
- They'll help you find a sponsoring rabbi

- They understand the unique challenges of converting in China and can provide long-term support

### 4. **Hebrew Learning**

Many Chabad Houses offer Hebrew classes or can recommend resources for Mandarin speakers learning Hebrew.

## Practical Information

### Kosher Food

In addition to Chabad House restaurants, you can find kosher products at:

- **Imported food stores** in major cities (check for kosher certification symbols)
- **Online**: Some websites deliver kosher products across China
- **Chabad Houses**: Often sell basic kosher staples (wine, matzah, challah)

### Jewish Holidays in China

Major Jewish holidays that Chabad celebrates publicly in China:

- **Rosh Hashanah** (Jewish New Year, Sept/Oct) — Services and festive meals
- **Yom Kippur** (Day of Atonement, Sept/Oct) — All-day fasting and prayer
- **Sukkot** (Feast of Tabernacles, Sept/Oct) — Building and eating in a sukkah
- **Chanukah** (Festival of Lights, Nov/Dec) — Public menorah lightings
- **Purim** (Festival of Esther, Feb/Mar) — Costume parties, Megillah reading
- **Passover** (Festival of Freedom, March/April) — Multi-course Seder meals

**Note**: Chabad in China often hosts the largest public Passover Seders in Asia, with hundreds of attendees. Reservations are required and typically open months in advance.

### Travel and Accommodations

If you're traveling to China for a Jewish experience:

- Some Chabad Houses can arrange **Shabbat hospitality** (a place to stay for Shabbat)
- They can also recommend nearby kosher-friendly hotels or apartments

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I have to be Jewish to visit?

**No.** Chabad welcomes everyone. If you're considering conversion, married to a Jew, or simply curious, you're invited.

### Will they try to make me more religious?

**No pressure.** Chabad's approach is educational, not coercive. They'll teach you, invite you, and support you — but the choice of how (or whether) to practice is entirely yours.

### I don't speak Hebrew or know any prayers. Is that okay?

**Absolutely.** Most attendees at Chabad Houses in China are beginners. Prayer books have transliterations and translations.

### Is it expensive?

**No.** Shabbat meals and holiday events are typically free or donation-based. Torah classes are free. Only special services (like catering a private event) have a cost.

### Can I bring my non-Jewish partner or friends?

**Yes.** Chabad is family-friendly and welcoming to all.

### I'm considering conversion. Where do I start?

**Contact a Chabad rabbi.** Explain your interest, and they'll guide you through the process. Conversion is a serious, multi-year commitment, but Chabad rabbis are experienced and supportive.

### What if I live in a city without a Chabad House?

**Reach out to the nearest one.** They can connect you with online classes, traveling rabbis, or other Jews in your area. You can also observe Jewish practices at home using online resources.

## Why Chabad Matters in China

Living as a Jew — or learning about Judaism — in a country where the religion is virtually unknown can feel isolating. Chabad breaks that isolation.

It's not just about access to kosher food or a place to pray. It's about **connection**: to a 4,000-year-old tradition, to a global community, and to others walking the same path.

For Chinese learners, Chabad represents a bridge. Judaism and Chinese culture both value education, family, moral living, and reverence for the past. At a Chabad House, you'll find rabbis who respect your heritage and can help you explore how Jewish wisdom speaks to your life.

For expatriates and travelers, Chabad is a home away from home — a place where you can celebrate your identity even thousands of miles from where you grew up.
