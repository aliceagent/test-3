# Bibliography - Torah Light Article #123: Minyan

## Primary Sources

### Talmudic Sources
1. **Talmud Bavli, Megillah 23b**
   - URL: https://www.sefaria.org/Megillah.23b
   - Description: Primary source establishing the requirement of ten men for a minyan from the "evil congregation" of the ten scouts
   - Language: Hebrew/Aramaic with English translation available

2. **Talmud Bavli, Berakhot 8a**
   - URL: https://www.sefaria.org/Berakhot.8a
   - Description: "God never rejects the prayer of a congregation" - establishing the spiritual efficacy of communal prayer
   - Language: Hebrew/Aramaic with English translation

### Biblical Sources
3. **Numbers 13-14 (Bamidbar)**
   - URL: https://www.sefaria.org/Numbers.13
   - Description: The account of the twelve scouts and the ten who gave an evil report about the Land of Israel
   - Language: Hebrew with multiple translations

4. **Genesis 18:32 (Bereishit)**
   - URL: https://www.sefaria.org/Genesis.18.32
   - Description: Abraham's negotiation with God regarding ten righteous people in Sodom
   - Language: Hebrew with multiple translations

## Halakhic Sources

5. **Shulchan Aruch, Orach Chaim 55**
   - URL: https://www.sefaria.org/Shulchan_Arukh%2C_Orach_Chayim.55
   - Description: Comprehensive laws regarding minyan requirements and applications
   - Author: Rabbi Joseph Karo (16th century)
   - Language: Hebrew

6. **Mishnah Berurah on Orach Chaim 55**
   - URL: https://www.sefaria.org/Mishnah_Berurah.55
   - Description: Detailed commentary and practical applications of minyan laws
   - Author: Rabbi Israel Meir Kagan (Chofetz Chaim)
   - Language: Hebrew

7. **Rambam (Maimonides), Mishneh Torah, Hilchot Tefillah 8**
   - URL: https://www.sefaria.org/Mishneh_Torah%2C_Prayer_and_the_Priestly_Blessing.8
   - Description: Maimonides' codification of prayer laws including minyan requirements
   - Language: Hebrew
