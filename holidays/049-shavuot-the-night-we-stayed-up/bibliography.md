## Additional Classical References (Traditional Jewish Sources)

- **Zohar** — Primary text of Jewish mysticism; earliest source mentioning the custom of Tikkun Leil Shavuot
- **Shulchan Aruch** — Rabbi Joseph Caro's codification of Jewish law
- **Magen Avraham** — 1692 codification of the custom in halachic literature
- **Talmud, Tractate Avodah Zarah 3b** — Source on the merit of nighttime Torah study
- **Song of Songs 4:11** — Biblical source for connecting Torah to milk and honey
- **Exodus 24:7** — Source for "We will do and we will listen"

## Historical Context Notes

- **Torah Given at Mount Sinai**: ~1300-1260 BCE
- **Chinese Dynasty Equivalent**: Late Shang Dynasty (商朝晚期)
- **Custom Formalized**: 16th century, Safed, Israel
- **Halachic Codification**: 1692, Magen Avraham
